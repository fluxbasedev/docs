title: Listing your first bot
description: A step by step guide to getting your bot listed on Fluxbase.
---

## Before you start

Before listing your bot, make sure you have the following ready:

- Your bot's Fluxer application ID
- A description of your bot (what it does, its features, etc.)
- An invite link for your bot
- A command prefix (if applicable)

<Note>
  Invite links that include the Administrator permission are not allowed on Fluxbase. It's best practice to only request the permissions your bot actually needs to function.
</Note>

## Step 1 - Find your bot

Head to the [Add your bot](https://fluxbase.dev/bot/new) page and enter your bot's application ID. Fluxbase will look up your bot and confirm it exists on Fluxer before proceeding.

## Step 2 - Fill out your bot's details

Once your bot has been found, you'll be asked to fill out some details about it:

- **Summary** - a short one-liner that appears on your bot's listing card
- **Description** - a full description of your bot, supports rich text formatting
- **Prefix** - your bot's command prefix
- **Categories** - the categories that best describe your bot
- **Invite URL** - the link used to add your bot to a server
- **Support server, website and repository** - optional but recommended

## Step 3 - Verify ownership

To confirm you own the bot, Fluxbase will generate a unique verification code. You'll need to paste this code into your bot's bio on Fluxer, then click **Complete Verification**.

Once verified, you can remove the code from your bot's bio.

<Note>
  The verification code expires after 7 days. If it expires before you verify, you can generate a new one from your dashboard.
</Note>

## Step 4 - Submit for review

Once your bot is verified, you can submit it to the review queue. A Fluxbase reviewer will test your bot and check it meets the [Platform Guidelines](https://fluxbase.dev/legal/guidelines) before approving it.

<Note>
  Submissions are reviewed in the order they are received. You can check your submission status at any time from your dashboard.
</Note>

## What happens after approval?

Once approved, your bot will be publicly listed on Fluxbase and discoverable by the community. If your submission is rejected, you'll be notified with a reason and can make the necessary changes before resubmitting.
